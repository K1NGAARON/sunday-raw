Automate customer referrals: Zolar sends 800 “Thank-you” boxes to new customers every month

Challenges for the customer

How can various departments handle merchandise more efficiently?

Sunday's solution

Set up different closets within the wardrobe so that each department can have its own campaigns and merch, while still enjoying the best group benefits.

Industry: Renewable Energy Semiconductor Manufacturing
Company size: +500 employees
Topic: Customer Loyalty Gifts

Background:

Zolar, a leading German solar energy provider, recognized the need to enhance the efficiency of their merchandise distribution across various departments, including marketing and HR. Operating with a network of technicians, they faced challenges in managing personalized merchandise usage within different teams.

Challenges:

Zolar experienced difficulties with interdepartmental sharing of merchandise, leading to inconsistencies and potential logistical issues. They also had a logistical problem: 800 packages had to be sent almost every month, which requires a lot of time and project management.The company needed a solution that would streamline the process, ensure each department had access to its own merchandise, and maintain a cohesive visual identity across all items.

The Sunday Solution:

Zolar turned to Sunday, to address these challenges and optimize their merchandise management. Sunday introduced the concept of their platform “the Sunday Wardrobe" to create a more organized and efficient system for handling Zolar's diverse merchandise needs.

Implementation:

The Sunday Wardrobe offered a customized solution for Zolar, featuring multiple closets within the wardrobe. Each department, including marketing and HR, now has its dedicated closet for merchandise. This not only ensures that every team has quick access to their specific items but also resolves the issue of merchandise being inadvertently shared between departments.

Benefits:

  1. Enhanced Departmental Autonomy: The Sunday Wardrobe provided Zolar's HR and marketing teams with autonomy over their respective merchandise, reducing conflicts and facilitating smoother internal operations.
  2. Scale Benefits: By consolidating their merchandise orders through the Sunday Wardrobe, Zolar benefited from economies of scale. Bulk ordering allowed for cost savings and more efficient logistics.
  3. Streamlined Process: The customized wardrobe solution streamlined the ordering and distribution process, making it more efficient and reducing the time spent on managing merchandise logistics.
  4. Cohesive Visual Identity: All items within the Sunday Wardrobe share a cohesive visual identity, reinforcing Zolar's brand consistency across various departments.

Result:

Zolar's collaboration with Sunday resulted in a more organized, efficient, and visually consistent approach to merchandise management. The implementation of the Sunday Wardrobe resolved the logistic challenges of shipping 800 packages a month and made sure each department can easily use merch for their campaign, but also provided Zolar with a scalable solution that aligns with their commitment to sustainability and excellence in solar energy.
