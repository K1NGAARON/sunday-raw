How to Design Eye-Catching New Employee Welcome Boxes

Read the full blog

Starting a new job can be like the first day of school all over again: exciting, but also nerve-wracking! That's where new employee welcome boxes come into play, turning those jitters into joy. These boxes are not just parcels; they're a hug in a box, a high-five, a warm welcome to the team. So, how do you design welcome boxes that stand out, make a statement, and genuinely make your new hires feel like part of the family? Let’s dive in the design advice and tips to create the most eye-catching new employee welcome boxes ever!

Part 1: Know Your Audience and Goals

Before you even pick out what color tissue paper to use, take a step back. Who are your new hires? What makes your team tick? Understanding your audience is the first step to designing a welcome box that resonates. Your goal? To make them feel seen, valued, and pumped about their new role.

Tip: Tailor the box contents to reflect your company culture. If you’re all about sustainability, opt for eco-friendly items. If your team bonds over coffee, include a branded mug. Let the box tell the story of who you are as a team.

Part 2: Make It Personal, Make It Pop

Now, let's get into the nitty-gritty of making those new employee welcome boxes unforgettable. It's all in the details - the personal touches that show you're not just any company; you're the company to be with.

Design Advice:

  • Color and Branding: Use your company colors boldly and creatively. A cohesive color scheme and clear branding will not only make your box pop but also strengthen brand identity from day one.
  • Just slapping a logo on mediocre items just doesn’t do it anymore. You need really customized items in your exact company colors and perfect branding.
  • Unboxing Experience: Think of the unboxing process as a journey. Each layer should reveal something exciting. Use branded tissue paper, stickers, and a layout that guides them through their goodies with anticipation.

Tip: Include a personal welcome note from the CEO or team members. A few heartfelt sentences can transform the box from cool to unforgettable, making it feel like a personal gift rather than a standard issue.

Part 3: Balance Between Fun and Functional

The best new employee welcome boxes strike a perfect balance between items that are fun (hello, company swag!) and items that are downright useful. You want your new hires to feel excited and equipped.

Design Advice:

  • Quality Over Quantity: Choose items that are durable and practical. A high-quality Backpack or a sleek, insulated water bottle can make a better impression than a dozen cheaper items.
  • Swag With Swagger: When it comes to branded apparel, think trendy yet timeless. A soft, well-designed t-shirt or a stylish cap can become favorites they actually want to wear, turning them into brand ambassadors outside the office too. Our design team can help you with that.
  • Never design it as workwear, this is a totally different category. Go for items and design styles that you yourself would wear on a Sunday.

Tip: Sneak in something unexpected that sparks joy. Whether it’s a quirky pair of socks or a fun company mascot, a little surprise can make a big difference in making your welcome box memorable.

Part 4: Packaging With a Punch

The box itself and how everything is packed can turn a simple welcome gift into a wow moment. This is where you can get creative with design elements and packaging materials to create an Instagram-worthy experience.

Design Advice:

  • Think Outside the Box: Consider custom packaging that reflects your brand's personality. A box with an inspiring message on the inside lid or a playful pattern can make a strong visual impact.
  • Eco-Chic: Eco-friendly packaging doesn’t have to be bland. Use recycled materials, seed paper, or compostable fillers to show your commitment to the planet in style.
  • Your own printed silk paper is a small investment for an amazing impact, it upgrades the whole experience to a new level.

Tip: Remember, the goal is to make them feel special and excited to be part of the team. A well-designed new employee welcome box can set the tone for their journey with your company, so invest time in making it thoughtful and unique.

Creating new employee welcome boxes that are eye-catching and meaningful is an art. It’s about blending practicality with personality, utility with uniqueness. By following these tips and putting your heart into the design, you can make every new hire's welcome feel like a celebration. After all, these boxes are more than just gifts; they're the beginning of a beautiful journey together. So let’s make those first steps unforgettable!

Frequently asked question

How can I determine the best items to include in a new employee welcome box that aligns with my company's unique culture?

To ensure your welcome box aligns with your company's culture, start by identifying the core values and themes that define your organization. Consider items that embody these principles, such as eco-friendly products for a company committed to sustainability, or sporty items for a company with a focus on health. Additionally, think about what makes your team unique and choose items that celebrate those characteristics, ensuring new hires feel a true connection to your company's identity from day one.

What are some tips for personalizing welcome boxes for remote employees to make them feel truly part of the team, despite the physical distance?

Personalizing welcome boxes for remote employees can involve including a personal welcome note from the CEO or team, definitely some wearable merchandise so during video calls they have a connection with their colleagues. Make sure they have the full starters kit to really feel part of the team from day one.

How can I ensure the packaging of the new employee welcome box creates an unforgettable unboxing experience?

Creating an unforgettable unboxing experience starts with thoughtful packaging design that reflects your brand's personality. Use custom packaging with your company's colors and logo, and consider adding inspiring messages or fun designs inside the box lid. Layer the items with branded tissue paper and include surprise elements, like personalized notes, to discover. The goal is to make unboxing an event in itself, leaving a lasting impression and building excitement about joining the team.
