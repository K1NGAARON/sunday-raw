The 5 Most Common Challenges When Scaling Branded Merchandise (and How to Tackle Them)

Scaling branded merchandise is a critical part of growing your brand, but it comes with its own set of challenges. From maintaining brand consistency across global markets to managing the complexities of logistics, each step in scaling can present hurdles that, if not properly addressed, can lead to inefficiencies, increased costs, and a diluted brand image.

At Sunday, we've identified the five most common challenges companies face when scaling their branded merchandise operations and developed effective strategies to overcome them. Here's how we can help ensure your merchandise operations are efficient, consistent, and deliver maximum impact.

1. Maintaining Brand Consistency Across All Merchandise, Departments, and Locations

The Challenge:
As your brand expands, maintaining consistent branding across various merchandise items, departments, and global locations becomes increasingly difficult. Without a centralized approach, you risk ending up with merchandise that doesn't match in color, quality, or design, leading to a disjointed brand experience. Different suppliers, different vendors, and inconsistent design processes can result in a patchwork of items that fail to represent your brand uniformly.

Our Solution:
To ensure that your brand remains consistent no matter where your merchandise is distributed, centralize your merchandise production with a single supplier capable of handling global logistics. This approach guarantees that all your merchandise—whether for an event in New York, a promotional campaign in Tokyo, or employee onboarding in Berlin—maintains the same color accuracy, quality standards, and design integrity.

At Sunday, we don't just design individual items as needed. Instead, we develop a comprehensive merchandise collection at the start of the year or quarter, aligned with your overall brand strategy. By creating this cohesive collection upfront, we ensure that all items—whether they're t-shirts, caps, or high-end jackets—are perfectly coordinated in terms of design and color. Our design team uses strict Pantone matching to translate your brand colors into every aspect of the merchandise, from screen prints to fabric dyes to embroidery threads. This meticulous process is supported by a detailed brand passport, which contains all the technical specifications needed to reproduce your brand consistently across different merchandise types.

Why This Matters:
Consistency in branding is crucial for building trust and recognition. When every item—from a small giveaway to a major promotional piece—reflects your brand accurately, it reinforces a consistent brand experience for your customers, employees, and partners. Whether someone in Sydney receives a branded hoodie or someone in London gets a pair of socks, the quality, color, and design will communicate the same message: that your brand is reliable, professional, and pays attention to detail.

2. Monitoring and Managing Merchandise Campaigns

The Challenge:
As your company scales, the management of merchandise often becomes decentralized. Different departments might have their own budgets, vendors, and design preferences, leading to a lack of oversight and coordination. This decentralization can result in missed opportunities to measure the impact of merchandise, untracked distribution, and ultimately, a loss of control over how your brand is represented.

Our Solution:
To address this challenge, Sunday developed the Wardrobe platform, a centralized system designed to streamline and manage all aspects of your merchandise operations. The platform allows you to organize your stock into virtual warehouses or closets, each assigned to different departments or campaigns. This system provides a comprehensive overview of what merchandise is being used, by whom, and for what purpose, ensuring that nothing slips through the cracks.

Our platform also enables you to track the effectiveness of your campaigns. For example, if your HR department distributes onboarding boxes to new employees, you can monitor exactly who received them, what items were included, and even gather feedback through automated NPS (Net Promoter Score) surveys. This data-driven approach allows you to measure the impact of your merchandise on brand ambassadorship and customer satisfaction. Additionally, our system supports stock moves, enabling you to reallocate unused merchandise from one department to another, ensuring that nothing goes to waste and that every item is used to its full potential.

Why This Matters:
Without proper monitoring, merchandise can easily become a sunk cost with no measurable return on investment. By centralizing management and tracking the distribution and effectiveness of your merchandise, you can ensure that every piece serves its intended purpose and contributes to your overall marketing and branding goals. This not only optimizes the use of resources but also provides valuable insights that can inform future campaigns.

3. Managing Global Logistics Effectively

The Challenge:
When your brand operates on a global scale, coordinating the logistics of shipping merchandise to various locations becomes a complex and daunting task. Ensuring that items arrive on time, clear customs, and reach their final destination without issues is critical, but challenging. Any delay or mishap in logistics can disrupt campaigns, disappoint customers, and lead to unnecessary costs.

Our Solution:
Sunday specializes in managing global logistics, ensuring that your merchandise reaches its destination efficiently and reliably. We categorize our logistics services into two main types: bulk shipments for large events and high-volume small package distributions. Whether you need to send thousands of t-shirts to a trade show in Las Vegas or distribute personalized onboarding kits to new hires across different continents, we handle the entire process.

Bulk shipments require careful planning, from managing customs clearance to ensuring that items arrive at the event venue on time. On the other hand, distributing high volumes of small packages involves more detailed logistics, including collecting accurate recipient information, managing preferences like size and color, and ensuring timely delivery. We understand that logistics is not just about shipping; it's about handling the entire distribution process, from beginning to end. Our experience and expertise allow us to optimize costs, streamline processes, and deliver your merchandise where and when it's needed—without the headaches of dealing with international logistics yourself.

Why This Matters:
Effective logistics management is crucial for maintaining the flow of your campaigns and ensuring that your merchandise has the desired impact. By partnering with a logistics expert like Sunday, you can focus on your core business while we handle the complexities of global distribution. This not only saves you time and resources but also guarantees that your merchandise operations run smoothly and efficiently.

4. Managing Costs While Maximizing Impact

The Challenge:
As merchandise operations scale, managing costs becomes increasingly important. Decentralized purchasing often leads to fragmented budgets, with different departments placing small, frequent orders. This not only drives up costs but also creates inefficiencies, as multiple employees must manage the purchasing process, leading to a significant drain on internal resources.

Our Solution:
Centralizing your merchandise operations with Sunday allows you to streamline the ordering process, reduce costs, and maximize the impact of your merchandise. We work with you to combine orders across departments, enabling you to benefit from economies of scale. By consolidating orders, we reduce the cost per unit and simplify the procurement process, saving your internal teams countless hours.

Moreover, by centralizing orders, we can proactively communicate with all departments to ensure that everyone's needs are met without duplication or unnecessary spending. This not only optimizes pricing but also ensures that your merchandise strategy is cohesive and aligned with your brand's goals. By centralizing your merchandise operations, you can achieve significant cost savings while maintaining the high quality and consistency that your brand demands.

Why This Matters:
Efficient cost management is essential for maximizing the return on your merchandise investment. By centralizing operations and reducing internal inefficiencies, you not only save money but also ensure that your merchandise efforts are focused and impactful. This approach allows you to get the most out of your budget while delivering high-quality merchandise that resonates with your audience.

5. Effective Inventory Management and Waste Reduction

The Challenge:
Poor inventory management can lead to excess stock, wasted resources, and ultimately, financial losses. In a decentralized system, leftover merchandise from various departments often gets lost, forgotten, or becomes obsolete due to rebranding or strategy changes. This not only represents wasted potential but also ties up capital in dead stock that could have been used more effectively elsewhere.

Our Solution:
At Sunday, we focus on maximizing the ROI of your merchandise by ensuring that every item is used effectively. Our team actively monitors your inventory and works with you to deploy stock strategically. For example, if a department has surplus t-shirts after an event, we can help reallocate them to another campaign or department, ensuring that nothing goes to waste.

Our approach to inventory management goes beyond just tracking stock levels; we actively seek opportunities to deploy unused merchandise in ways that enhance your brand's visibility and impact. Whether it's redistributing stock to new locations, repurposing items for different campaigns, or even offering limited edition giveaways, we ensure that every item in your inventory continues to generate value for your brand.

Why This Matters:
Effective inventory management is crucial for reducing waste and maximizing the return on your merchandise investment. By ensuring that every item is used strategically, you can avoid the pitfalls of dead stock and make the most of your resources. This not only reduces costs but also ensures that your merchandise continues to work for you long after it's been produced.

Scaling your branded merchandise operations doesn't have to be a daunting task. By addressing these common challenges head-on and partnering with a trusted supplier like Sunday, you can ensure that your merchandise operations are efficient, consistent, and impactful. Whether you're looking to maintain brand consistency, streamline logistics, or maximize ROI, our team is here to help you succeed every step of the way.
